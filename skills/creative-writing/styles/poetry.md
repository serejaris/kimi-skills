# Poetry & Lyrics Style

Verse — 诗/词/现代诗, sonnets, haiku, and 歌词/lyrics. Compression and sound are the craft.

## Voice and Tone
- Image and music over statement. A poem earns its feeling through concrete particulars, not by naming the emotion.
- Honor the requested form precisely: classical 律诗/绝句 (平仄、对仗、押韵、字数), sonnet (meter, rhyme scheme), haiku (5-7-5 / seasonal), or free verse.

## Structure & Format
- **Classical Chinese verse**: obey the form's 格律 — line length, tonal pattern, 对仗, and rhyme. Don't break the form unless asked.
- **Lyrics**: verse / pre-chorus / chorus / bridge structure; the chorus is the memorable, repeatable core; keep syllable counts singable and consistent across parallel sections; rhyme that sounds natural sung, not forced.
- **Free verse**: line breaks are meaning — break for emphasis, breath, and turn. Stanzas group thought/image.

## Language Patterns
- Concrete, surprising images; trust the reader to feel without being told.
- Sound matters: rhythm, assonance, internal rhyme; read aloud and tune.
- Specific over grand — the small true detail carries more than cosmic abstraction.

## Prohibited Patterns
- Clichéd imagery ("tapestry of emotions", 星辰大海堆砌, generic nature/love metaphors).
- Forced profundity or forced rhyme that distorts sense.
- Over-rhyming; abstract emotion with no grounding image.
- Breaking the requested fixed form (格律/meter) without reason.
