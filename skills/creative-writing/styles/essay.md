# Essay, Prose & Letter Style

散文/随笔/议论文, personal and literary prose, and 书信/letters. Here a genuine voice and a real line of thought matter more than "show, don't tell."

## Voice and Tone
- A real, particular voice — opinions, doubts, a point of view. First person is honest here, not unprofessional.
- Tone fits the occasion: reflective for 随笔, warm for a personal letter, rigorous for 议论文. For letters, address the recipient directly and hold the relationship in mind.

## Structure & Format
- **散文/随笔**: associative but shaped — a controlling image or idea the piece circles and deepens. Earn the ending; don't summarize it.
- **议论文**: a clear claim, real reasons, concrete evidence/examples, honest engagement with the counter-case. Logic carries it.
- **Letter**: salutation → body (one or two real things, said directly) → close. Match formality to the relationship.

## Language Patterns
- Concrete particulars over abstraction — the specific memory, the named thing.
- Vary rhythm; let a short sentence land a point.
- Think on the page: react to ideas, allow nuance and mixed feeling.

## Prohibited Patterns
- Throat-clearing openings ("在当今这个快节奏的时代…", "In today's fast-paced world…").
- Summarizing, bow-tying endings ("总而言之", "The future looks bright").
- 首先…其次…最后 scaffolding, 综上所述, and the AI-essay 腔.
- Hollow uplift with no specific substance behind it.
