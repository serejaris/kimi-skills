# Audio Drama (有声剧 / 广播剧) Style

Audio-only narrative — everything reaches the listener through **voice, dialogue, narration, and sound**. Nothing visual unless spoken or implied by sound.

## Voice and Tone
- Carry story through dialogue and a narrator (旁白). The narrator bridges what can't be dramatized; trim it to what the ear needs, keep key dialogue intact.
- Voices must be distinguishable by ear alone — diction, rhythm, verbal tics — since there are no faces.

## Structure & Format
- Episode-based; honor the user's episode count and per-episode length.
- Per line, label the source: `【旁白】`, `【角色名】`, and sound cues `【音效：…】` / `【BGM：…】` (follow the user's instruction or sample format if provided).
- Open each episode with a quick re-grounding (where/who) and end on a hook to pull the listener to the next.
- Preserve the source's important plot and key dialogue when adapting; compress description into narration or sound, don't drop story.

## Language Patterns
- Speakable lines — read them aloud in your head; cut tongue-twisters and over-long sentences.
- Use sound to show, not tell: a slammed door, rain, a held breath — written as cues, not described in prose.
- Distinguish speakers through how they talk, not narrator labels alone.

## Prohibited Patterns
- Visual-only description the listener can't perceive ("she glances at the photo on the wall" with no audio anchor).
- Narration that smothers the scene — over-explaining what dialogue/sound already conveys.
- Identical-sounding characters; losing canon plot/dialogue in adaptation.
