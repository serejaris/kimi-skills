# Technical Report Style

## Voice and Tone
- Precise and methodology-transparent. The reader should be able to reproduce the analysis
- Objective: findings presented as outcomes of a defined process, not assertions
- Technical but not obscure: define specialized terms, spell out abbreviations on first use
- Systematic: follow a logical progression from problem to method to findings to implications

## Structure Conventions
- Problem definition → Methodology → Analysis → Results → Discussion → Limitations
- Every quantitative claim includes: the value, the unit, the measurement method, and the source
- Diagrams and schematics preferred over textual descriptions for systems and processes
- Use three-line tables for data presentation (academic convention)
- Clearly separate established knowledge from novel findings or interpretations

## Language Patterns
- Prefer: "Latency measured at 23.4ms ± 2.1ms (n=1000, p95)" over "latency was low"
- Prefer: "Using Algorithm X (Smith et al., 2023), we computed..." over "We calculated the results"
- Passive voice acceptable when the method matters more than the actor
- Version and configuration details for all tools, frameworks, and datasets used

## Prohibited Patterns
- Unsubstantiated claims: "this approach is superior" without comparative evidence
- Missing methodology: any result without explanation of how it was obtained
- Ambiguous precision: "approximately 50%" when exact figures are available
