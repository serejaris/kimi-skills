# Industry Research Report Style

## Voice and Tone
- Data-driven and analytical. Every claim is backed by quantitative evidence
- Forward-looking: describe current state, then project implications and trends
- Authoritative but measured. Use hedged language for forecasts: "projected to," "estimated at," "on track to"
- Third-person perspective throughout. No "we believe" or "I think"

## Structure Conventions
- Lead each section with the most important finding, then support with evidence
- Heavy use of comparative analysis: market share tables, growth rate comparisons, competitive positioning
- Each major claim should be supported within 1–2 sentences by a data point with citation
- Forecast sections clearly separated from historical analysis
- Use "base case / bull case / bear case" framing for projections when appropriate

## Language Patterns
- Prefer: "revenue grew 23% YoY to $4.2B[^N^]" over "revenue experienced strong growth"
- Prefer: "margin compression of 340bps driven by input cost inflation" over "margins declined"
- Quantify everything that can be quantified. If you can't quantify, explain why
- Financial terminology follows standard conventions: CAGR, YoY, QoQ, bps, EBITDA

## Prohibited Patterns
- Promotional language: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "unprecedented" (unless literally true and cited)
- Generic industry filler: "the market is witnessing rapid transformation"
- Unsourced forecasts: never project numbers without methodology or source attribution
