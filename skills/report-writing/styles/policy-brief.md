# Policy Brief Style

## Voice and Tone
- Evidence-based and neutral. Present all sides when evidence is contested
- Concise and pragmatic — every sentence earns its place
- Policy-literate: use standard policy terminology naturally
- Authoritative institutional voice, not personal commentary

## Structure Conventions
- Background → Current situation → Policy options → Recommendations → Implementation
- Recommendations tied directly to evidence presented in the analysis
- Use comparative policy analysis: what other jurisdictions have done and with what results
- Keep paragraphs short — policy readers scan before reading deeply
- Tables for policy option comparison (criteria: feasibility, cost, impact, timeline)

## Language Patterns
- Prefer: "The regulation took effect in Q3 2024, affecting an estimated 2,400 firms[^N^]" over "The new regulation impacts many businesses"
- Use precise policy terms: "fiscal multiplier," "regulatory arbitrage," "implementation gap"
- For Chinese government contexts: adopt pragmatic, condensed language typical of policy memos (内参 style) — direct, factual, action-oriented, minimal rhetorical flourish

## Prohibited Patterns
- Advocacy disguised as analysis: "clearly, the best approach is..."
- Vague policy recommendations: "the government should take action" without specifying what action
- Ignoring trade-offs: every policy option must discuss both benefits and costs
