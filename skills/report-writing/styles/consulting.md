# Consulting Deliverable Style

## Voice and Tone
- Insight-oriented: lead with "so what" before presenting evidence
- Action-focused: every analysis section should point toward a decision or recommendation
- Client-facing: professional but accessible, avoiding unnecessary jargon
- Confident but not arrogant. Present findings as evidence-based conclusions, not opinions

## Structure Conventions
- Pyramid principle: conclusion first, then supporting arguments, then details
- Each section answers a specific business question
- Recommendations are concrete and actionable: who does what by when
- Use "situation → complication → resolution" narrative arcs for problem-framing sections
- Executive summary must be self-contained: a busy reader should get full value from it alone

## Language Patterns
- Prefer: "Three factors drive this shift:" over lengthy introductory paragraphs
- Prefer: "We recommend prioritizing X because [evidence]" over "It may be worth considering X"
- Use numbered lists for sequential actions or ranked priorities
- Business terminology: ROI, TCO, time-to-market, scalability, unit economics

## Prohibited Patterns
- Academic hedging: "it could potentially be argued that" — state the finding directly
- Passive recommendations: "improvements could be made" — specify who, what, how
- Analysis without implication: never end a section with data alone — state what it means for the client
