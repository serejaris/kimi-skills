# Survey / Literature Review Style

## Voice and Tone
- Comprehensive and synthesizing. The goal is to organize a field, not just list papers
- Authoritative: demonstrate command of the landscape through thoughtful categorization
- Balanced: give fair treatment to competing approaches, note trade-offs objectively
- Forward-looking: identify trends, open problems, and future directions

## Structure Conventions
- Organize by **theme or methodology**, not chronologically
- Each thematic section: define the approach category → key representative works → strengths/limitations → evolution over time
- Comparison tables are essential: compare 5-10+ methods on consistent dimensions
- Include a taxonomy figure showing how approaches relate to each other
- Conclude each major thread with a gap analysis pointing to open problems

## Language Patterns
- Prefer: "Three paradigms have emerged: A[^1^][^2^], B[^3^][^4^], and C[^5^], each addressing different aspects of the problem" over listing papers one by one
- Prefer: "While A achieves strong results on X[^1^], it struggles with Y, which B addresses through Z[^3^]" over separate disconnected summaries
- Use transitional analysis between groups: explain why the field moved from approach A to B

## Prohibited Patterns
- Paper-by-paper enumeration without synthesis ("X did A. Y did B. Z did C.")
- Missing comparison: discussing methods without comparing them on shared dimensions
- Recency bias: ignoring foundational work that established key concepts
- Drive-by citations: mentioning a paper without substantive description (minimum 1-2 sentences per cited work)
