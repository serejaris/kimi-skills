# Case Study Paper Style

## Voice and Tone
- Narrative-driven but evidence-grounded. Tell the story of the case with analytical rigor
- Contextual: thick description of the setting, actors, and constraints
- Analytical: draw generalizable insights from specific observations
- Reflexive: acknowledge the researcher's role and perspective where relevant

## Structure Conventions
- Introduction: research question → why this case → theoretical framing
- Case description: context, timeline, key events, stakeholders
- Analysis: apply theoretical framework to case evidence, identify patterns
- Cross-case comparison (for multi-case studies): systematic comparison on defined dimensions
- Discussion: generalizability, boundary conditions, theoretical contributions
- Data sources should be triangulated (interviews, documents, observations)

## Language Patterns
- Prefer: "The organization adopted strategy X in Q3 2023 following regulatory pressure[^1^], resulting in a 15% cost reduction[^2^]" over "The organization made changes"
- Prefer: "Three themes emerged from analysis: A, B, and C, with A appearing in 8 of 10 interviews" over "Several themes were identified"
- Ground claims in evidence: always trace insights back to specific data

## Prohibited Patterns
- Anecdotes without analysis: telling the story without extracting insights
- Overgeneralization: "This case proves that..." — single cases suggest, they don't prove
- Missing theoretical grounding: describing events without an analytical framework
- Selective evidence: presenting only data that supports the argument, ignoring contradictions
