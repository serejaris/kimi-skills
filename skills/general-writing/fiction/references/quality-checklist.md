# Quality Checklist

Per-chapter quality gate for fiction writers and review editors. Aligned with Stage 3 review pipeline.

---

## Opening (first 20%)

- [ ] Immediate conflict, tension, or dramatic question within the first 3 paragraphs
- [ ] No banned openings: weather description, daily routine, recap of previous chapter, slow exposition dump, empty pleasantry dialogue
- [ ] Hook-in picks up from previous chapter's hook-out (or establishes fresh entry for Ch 1)
- [ ] Setting grounded in 2+ senses within the first page
- [ ] POV character's desire or problem stated or implied early

## Plot & Scene

- [ ] Chapter contains at least one irreversible event — something changes that cannot be undone
- [ ] Every scene follows goal → conflict → outcome (disaster or success with complication)
- [ ] No "nothing happens" connector chapters — if a chapter exists, it earns its place
- [ ] Cause-and-effect chain: each event triggers the next; no coincidence-driven plot
- [ ] Information reveals are dramatized (shown in scene), not reported in summary
- [ ] Subplots advance or are referenced — no thread goes dormant more than 3 chapters

## Character

- [ ] Behavior consistent with established personality, or deviation is explained within the chapter
- [ ] Characters react to events with emotion AND action — never passive observers
- [ ] At least one moment of internal conflict or difficult choice per POV chapter
- [ ] Character voice distinguishable in dialogue (cover names, identify speaker test)
- [ ] Relationships shift — no static interactions between the same characters across chapters
- [ ] Secondary characters serve a narrative function; no decorative presences

## Dialogue

- [ ] Every line advances plot, reveals character, or creates conflict — cut if it does none
- [ ] Subtext present in >= 30% of exchanges (characters say one thing, mean another)
- [ ] Action beats replace dialogue tags where possible ("he said" < "he slammed the folder shut")
- [ ] No on-the-nose dialogue: characters don't explain feelings they'd normally suppress
- [ ] Distinct speech patterns per character: vocabulary, sentence length, verbal tics, topics avoided
- [ ] Exposition through dialogue feels natural — no "as you know, Bob" information dumps

## Hook & Suspense

- [ ] Chapter has both hook-in (opening grab) and hook-out (closing compel)
- [ ] Hook type differs from previous chapter's hook-out (no consecutive same-type hooks)
- [ ] At least one old tension closed or advanced per chapter while a new one opens
- [ ] Hook intensity appropriate to story position (rising across the volume arc)
- [ ] Hook intensity escalation on 5-level scale: (1) curiosity → (2) concern → (3) urgency → (4) survival → (5) existential. Sagging intensity across 3+ consecutive chapters is a pacing defect

**Hook prohibitions**:
- [ ] No false suspense resolving as misunderstanding ("it was just the cat")
- [ ] No deus ex machina via unestablished capabilities
- [ ] No low-stakes cliffhangers with trivial consequences
- [ ] No dangling hooks — each chapter must close at least one old hook while opening a new one

## Show vs Tell

- [ ] Emotions rendered through physical sensation, action, or dialogue — not named directly
- [ ] Setting conveyed through character interaction with environment, not catalog description
- [ ] Backstory woven into present action (flashback max 2 paragraphs unless it IS the scene)
- [ ] Adjectives earn their place — no stacking ("beautiful, elegant, graceful")
- [ ] Key scenes are dramatized in real time; only skip-worthy transitions are summarized

Common tell → show patterns:

| Tell (cut) | Show (use) |
|------------|-----------|
| He was angry | His knuckles whitened around the glass stem |
| She felt nervous | She adjusted her glasses for the third time |
| The room was messy | Takeout boxes stacked on the desk, a shirt draped over the monitor |
| He was wealthy | He peeled bills from a roll without counting |
| 他很愤怒 | 他握紧拳头，指节发白 |
| 她很紧张 | 她反复调整眼镜的位置 |

## Pacing & Rhythm

- [ ] Sentence length varies — no 3+ consecutive sentences of similar length
- [ ] Paragraph length varies — action scenes use short paragraphs, reflection uses longer ones
- [ ] Information density alternates: high (action/dialogue) ↔ low (description/interiority)
- [ ] Tension rises and releases within the chapter — not a flat line
- [ ] Scene breaks mark genuine shifts in time, location, or POV — not arbitrary pauses
- [ ] Scene pacing follows goal → conflict → outcome (disaster or success with complication)
- [ ] No sagging middle — mid-chapter pacing must sustain momentum
- [ ] No rushed climax — climactic moments receive proportional page time
- [ ] Action/reflection ratio appropriate for genre (action-heavy genres: >= 60% action scenes; literary: flexible)

## Sensory & World

- [ ] Each scene engages >= 3 senses, at least 1 non-visual
- [ ] Setting details serve mood or character — no tourism-guide descriptions
- [ ] World rules established earlier in the story are not violated
- [ ] Props and objects introduced earlier are used consistently (Chekhov's gun discipline)
- [ ] Time of day, weather, season referenced only when they affect mood or action

## Language & Anti-AI

- [ ] No AI vocabulary clusters (English): "delve", "tapestry", "testament", "beacon", "landscape", "multifaceted", "resonate", "nuanced"
- [ ] No AI vocabulary clusters (Chinese fiction — **HARD FAIL** if 3+ instances per chapter): 某种, 微微, 嘴角微微上扬, 嘴角勾起一抹弧度, 不由自主, 空气仿佛凝固, 气氛一时之间, 声音沙哑/低沉/冰冷 (showing with "声音XX" template), 目光深邃/锐利/温柔 (showing with "目光XX" template)
- [ ] No hedging stacks: "somewhat", "perhaps", "it seemed as though"
- [ ] No four-character-idiom overuse (Chinese): max 2 per paragraph, never consecutive
- [ ] Simile density check: 像是/仿佛/好像 max 2 per paragraph, max 8 per chapter
- [ ] No copula avoidance patterns: artificially eliminating "is/was" creates worse prose than using them naturally
- [ ] Sentence structures varied — not repetitive SVO or topic-comment patterns
- [ ] No "不是X，是Y" template overuse: max 1 per chapter
- [ ] Voice matches the established tone and POV character's register
- [ ] Em-dash density ≤ 5 per 1000 Chinese chars (≤15 per 3000-char chapter). Count all "——" occurrences. **HARD FAIL** if exceeded — triggers automatic REVISE.
- [ ] No English word leakage in Chinese text: scan for isolated English words (>2 letters) embedded in Chinese prose. Common Claude artifacts: "additional", "beige", "handwriting", "scheme". **HARD FAIL** if found — rewrite the sentence in pure Chinese.

## Continuity

- [ ] Previous chapter's hook-out is addressed (not ignored or contradicted)
- [ ] Character physical state matches last known state (injuries, location, possessions)
- [ ] Timeline is consistent — hours/days elapsed are plausible
- [ ] Foreshadowing plants from earlier chapters are respected (check `foreshadowing.md`)
- [ ] No information the POV character shouldn't know leaks into narration

## Genre-Specific Checks

### Mystery / Thriller / Suspense (悬疑/惊悚)
- [ ] Clue or red herring planted per chapter
- [ ] Logic holds under scrutiny — no plot holes in the deduction chain
- [ ] Tension escalates chapter over chapter

### Romance (言情/爱情)
- [ ] Relationship advances or retreats — no stasis
- [ ] Emotional stakes are clear — reader cares about the pairing
- [ ] Physical/romantic beats are earned by prior emotional buildup

### Fantasy / Sci-Fi (奇幻/科幻)
- [ ] Magic/tech system rules are consistent with established canon
- [ ] World-building integrated into action, not info-dumped
- [ ] Power scaling is controlled — no sudden unexplained power jumps

### Xianxia / Wuxia (仙侠/武侠)
- [ ] Cultivation/martial arts progression follows established rules
- [ ] Combat choreography is spatially clear
- [ ] Sect/faction politics advance the plot, not just fill pages

### Literary Fiction (文学小说)
- [ ] Thematic resonance — the chapter's events illuminate the central question
- [ ] Prose style serves meaning, not decoration
- [ ] Ambiguity is deliberate, not accidental

### Action / Adventure (动作/冒险)
- [ ] Action sequences are spatially clear — reader can map the environment
- [ ] Stakes are physical AND personal
- [ ] Pacing is fast — short sentences, short paragraphs, momentum

## Scoring

Used by review editors. Each section scores 0-10. Weights:

| Dimension | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Opening | 10% |
| Plot & Scene | 15% |
| Character | 15% |
| Dialogue | 10% |
| Hook & Suspense | 10% |
| Show vs Tell | 10% |
| Pacing & Rhythm | 10% |
| Sensory & World | 5% |
| Language & Anti-AI | 10% |
| Continuity | 5% |

**Weighted total → Quality Gate**:
- >= 85: **PASS** — no action needed
- 75-84: **WARNING** — write a targeted fix brief (specific paragraphs, quoted issues, expected outcome) and dispatch a fresh subagent to apply. Re-score once.
- < 75: **REVISE** — write a detailed fix brief (file paths, quoted findings, before→after, scope boundary) and dispatch a fresh subagent to rewrite. Re-review after fix. Max 2 rewrite cycles.

**Escalation**: If a chapter fails REVISE twice (3 total attempts), apply FORCED pass. Add ALL remaining issues to the backpatch queue with severity tags (CONTINUITY_BREAK / CHARACTER_INCONSISTENCY / STYLE_ISSUE / AI_FINGERPRINT).

**Hard-fail dimensions** (ANY of these triggers automatic REVISE regardless of total score):
- Em-dash density > 5/千字
- English word leakage (any instance in Chinese prose)
- Character name contradiction vs. characters.md
- Timeline contradiction vs. chapter_summaries.md

## Assembly Checklist

Final verification before delivering the assembled work. `final_check_agent` runs this against the complete manuscript and satellite files.

- [ ] POV consistency (no unintentional head-hopping across chapters)
- [ ] Timeline coherence (verify against `chapter_summaries.md` — linear walk, no contradictions)
- [ ] Character voice distinctiveness (dialogue test: cover names, identify speaker)
- [ ] Chekhov's gun (verify `foreshadowing.md` — no OPEN or DANGLING items remaining)
- [ ] Opening hook strength (Ch 1 opening grabs within 3 paragraphs)
- [ ] Ending resonance (final chapter delivers on the story's promise)
- [ ] AI-pattern absence (full-text scan against anti-ai.md checklist)
- [ ] Scene economy (every scene advances plot, character, or theme — no filler)
- [ ] World consistency (verify against `world.md` — no rule violations across chapters)
- [ ] Hook system compliance (every chapter has hook-in and hook-out, no danglers)
- [ ] Character arcs complete (verify against `characters.md` — arcs reach projected end state)
- [ ] Sensory grounding (every scene: >= 3 senses, >= 1 non-visual)
- [ ] Volume arc integrity (each volume's conflict resolved, transitions between volumes smooth)
- [ ] Word count targets met (per-chapter and total)
